The switch uses spade connectors so I had to practice making those connections using a crimping tool which was fairly easy.

The switch has 6 quick connects. The middle 2 connectors are the output and the top and bottom two connectors are the 2 inputs you can switch with. In the above example I didn’t use all 6 connectors, but only 3. This is because I ran ground to both LED lights regardless of switch status.

Not needing to wire ground through the switch is important because when I strip a 3.5mm connection I should get a cable for the left channel, right channel and ground. This gives me just enough inputs on the switch to finish the job! (You can buy switches that support more though).
